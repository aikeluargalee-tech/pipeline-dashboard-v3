Treasury Yields: Risk-Free Rate Pressure on Bitcoin

Macro Context · 5 min read

The 10-year Treasury yield represents the "risk-free rate" — the return you get for lending to the US government with essentially zero risk. When this rate rises, risk assets like Bitcoin face pressure because the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding assets increases.

Dashboard Thresholds

  • Yield < 4.0%: Risk-on. Low opportunity cost — capital flows to risk assets including BTC.
  • Yield > 5.0%: Risk-off. High opportunity cost — capital flows from risk assets to bonds.
  • 4.0-5.0%: Neutral.

Why It Matters for Bitcoin

Bitcoin doesn't pay interest or dividends. When Treasury yields are high, investors can earn 5%+ risk-free by holding bonds. This makes Bitcoin's risk/reward less attractive — why take on BTC's volatility when you can earn 5% with essentially no risk?

Conversely, when yields are low, the opportunity cost of holding Bitcoin is minimal — capital flows to risk assets seeking higher returns.

In the Macro Regime

The 10-year yield is one of four signals in the macro regime classification. Yield < 4.0% adds a bullish signal; yield > 5.0% adds a bearish signal. Combined with DXY, VIX, and ETF flows, this produces the overall macro regime.
