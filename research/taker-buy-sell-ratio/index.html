Taker Buy/Sell Ratio: Real-Time Order Flow

Derivatives · 5 min read

The taker buy/sell ratio is the most immediate sentiment indicator in the dashboard. It measures aggressive buying vs selling in the last hour — who's crossing the spread to get filled right now.

What It Measures

Every trade has a taker (aggressive — crosses the spread) and a maker (passive — has a resting order). The taker buy/sell ratio divides taker buy volume by taker sell volume. Above 1.0 = buyers more aggressive. Below 1.0 = sellers more aggressive.

Dashboard Thresholds

  • > 1.2: Strong buy pressure. Buyers are aggressively lifting the ask.
  • 0.95-1.05: Balanced. No clear aggression from either side.
  • < 0.8: Strong sell pressure. Sellers are aggressively hitting the bid.

Role in Signal Detection

The taker ratio is a critical input for volume confirmation in signal monitors:

  • Breakout-Retest: Requires taker ratio > 1.2 AND CVD > 0 for volume confirmation. Both must agree — aggressive buyers AND positive cumulative flow.
  • Breakdown-Retest: Requires taker ratio < 0.8 AND CVD < 0. Both must confirm aggressive selling.

This dual confirmation prevents false signals from single-metric noise.

Thin Hours Filter

During thin hours (00:00-06:00 UTC), the taker ratio is less reliable — low volume means a few large trades can skew the ratio. The signal monitors enforce a minimum volume threshold (50 BTC per 15m candle) during these hours to filter out noise.
