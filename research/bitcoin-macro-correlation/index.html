Bitcoin and Macro: How DXY, Yields, and VIX Move BTC

Pillar article · Macro Context cluster · 9 min read

Bitcoin may be a decentralized currency, but it trades like a risk asset. When the dollar strengthens, BTC typically weakens. When equity volatility spikes, BTC sells off. The Pipeline Dashboard's Layer 1 tracks the macroeconomic environment that all risk assets operate within.

DXY (US Dollar Index)

The most reliable macro correlation for Bitcoin is the inverse relationship with the US Dollar Index. DXY < 100 creates a BTC tailwind; DXY > 105 creates a headwind. The dashboard classifies DXY as bullish (< 100), bearish (> 104), or neutral.

Treasury Yields

The 10-year Treasury yield represents the "risk-free rate." When yields rise, risk assets face pressure as the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding assets like Bitcoin increases. The dashboard flags yields < 4.0% as risk-on and > 5.0% as risk-off.

VIX (Volatility Index)

The VIX measures expected 30-day volatility in the S&P 500. It's the "fear gauge" — when VIX spikes, equity markets are stressed, and that stress spills over to Bitcoin. The dashboard uses VIX both in the Gate0 layer (> 30 = PAUSE, > 40 = ABORT) and in the macro layer (< 18 = risk-on, > 25 = risk-off).

ETF Flows

Since the approval of Bitcoin spot ETFs in January 2024, ETF flows have become a primary driver of BTC price. Daily net inflows indicate institutional capital entering; sustained outflows indicate institutional exit. The dashboard tracks daily and weekly net flows and classifies the trend as inflow, mild outflow, or heavy outflow.

M2 Money Supply

M2 measures broad money supply including cash, checking deposits, and savings. Expanding M2 is liquidity-positive for risk assets; contracting M2 is a headwind. The dashboard tracks M2 as a macro context indicator.

Risk Asset Correlation

The dashboard tracks SPY (S&P 500), QQQ (Nasdaq-100), and GLD (Gold) alongside BTC. During normal market conditions, BTC correlates positively with equities (especially QQQ). During acute risk-off events, correlations can break down — BTC may decouple for weeks.

Macro Regime Classification

The dashboard classifies the macro regime by counting bullish vs bearish signals across DXY, VIX, yields, and ETF flows:

  • Risk-on: ≥ 3 bullish signals (weak dollar, low VIX, low yields, ETF inflows)
  • Risk-off: ≥ 3 bearish signals (strong dollar, high VIX, high yields, ETF outflows)
  • Mild-risk-on/off: Lean in one direction but not extreme
  • Mixed: Conflicting signals, no clear direction
Correlation breakdown: During acute risk-off events (e.g., COVID March 2020), BTC can decouple from DXY/liquidity for weeks. The macro layer assumes BTC eventually re-correlates — but during breakdowns, structural and derivatives layers matter more.
