Support and Resistance: ATR-Weighted Levels

Structural Liquidity · 6 min read

Support and resistance levels are the backbone of technical analysis. But not all levels are equal — the Pipeline Dashboard weights them by ATR (Average True Range) to account for volatility, and computes them across three timeframes for confluence analysis.

Multi-Timeframe Approach

The dashboard computes S/R bands on three timeframes:

  • 1-hour: Intraday levels for short-term trading
  • 4-hour: Swing trading levels — the primary timeframe for signal detection
  • 1-day: Macro structure levels — long-term support/resistance

When levels align across timeframes (confluence), they're stronger. The dashboard shows how many timeframes are "active" — 3/3 means all timeframes have valid S/R data.

ATR Weighting

Each S/R level includes an ATR percentage — the level's distance from current price as a percentage of ATR. This normalizes for volatility: a level 1 ATR away in a high-volatility market is equivalent to a level 1 ATR away in a low-volatility market, even though the dollar distance differs.

Strength Labels

Each level has a strength label based on how many times price has reacted to it and the volume at that level. Stronger levels are more likely to hold; weaker levels are more likely to break.

How S/R Feeds Signal Detection

The 4-hour S/R levels are the primary input for the Breakout-Retest and Breakdown-Retest signal monitors. When price breaks above 4H resistance with volume confirmation, a breakout zone is created. When price breaks below 4H support, a breakdown zone is created.

Space requirement: The signal monitors require at least 0.5x ATR of space between the broken level and the next magnet/level. This prevents entries in compressed ranges where there's no room for the trade to work.
