CVD: Cumulative Volume Delta Explained

Derivatives · 7 min read

CVD (Cumulative Volume Delta) is the most direct measure of real-time buying and selling pressure. It tracks the cumulative difference between taker buy volume and taker sell volume — showing you who's actually winning the order flow battle.

What CVD Measures

Every trade has a taker (the one who crosses the spread) and a maker (the one with the resting order). Taker buys represent aggressive buying — someone willing to pay the ask price. Taker sells represent aggressive selling — someone willing to hit the bid.

CVD is the running sum of (taker buy volume - taker sell volume) over 24 hours. Positive CVD = buyers dominating. Negative CVD = sellers dominating.

The Dashboard Computation

The collect_derivatives() function fetches 24 hours of hourly taker buy/sell data from Binance Futures. It validates that at least 80% of candles have both buyVol and sellVol fields, then computes:

  • CVD 24h: Cumulative (buyVol - sellVol) across all valid candles
  • CVD trend: Compares the last 4 hours of delta to the first 4 hours

Reading the Trend

The trend matters more than the absolute value:

  • Strengthening: Recent buying pressure exceeds earlier buying. Buyers are getting more aggressive.
  • Weakening: Recent selling pressure exceeds earlier selling. Sellers are getting more aggressive.
  • Flat: Buy/sell pressure roughly balanced. No clear direction.

CVD in Signal Detection

CVD is a critical input for all three signal monitors:

  • VAL Absorption: Requires CVD holding (non-negative) — buyers still present despite price drop
  • Breakout-Retest: Requires CVD > 0 for volume confirmation — buyers supporting the breakout
  • Breakdown-Retest: Requires CVD < 0 — sellers supporting the breakdown. If CVD flips positive during retest, the signal is skipped (that's VAL Absorption territory)
The CVD flip filter: In Breakdown-Retest detection, if CVD turns positive during the retest phase, the signal is skipped with status "cvd_flip_skip." This prevents shorting when buyers have stepped in — that's a VAL Absorption setup, not a breakdown.
