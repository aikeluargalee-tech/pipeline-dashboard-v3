VIX and Bitcoin: Equity Volatility Spillover

Risk Frameworks · 6 min read

Bitcoin trades like a risk asset — when equity markets panic, BTC sells off. The VIX (CBOE Volatility Index) is the "fear gauge" that measures expected 30-day volatility in the S&P 500. The Pipeline Dashboard uses VIX in two places: the Gate0 risk gate and the macro regime classification.

VIX Thresholds in Gate0

  • VIX > 40 → ABORT: Extreme equity panic. BTC will be hit hard. Flatten everything.
  • VIX > 30 → PAUSE: Significant equity stress. No new BTC positions.
  • VIX > 22 → TIGHTENED: Elevated volatility. Reduce size.
  • VIX < 18 → Risk-on: Calm markets. Normal trading.

VIX in the Macro Layer

In the macro regime classification, VIX contributes to the risk-on/risk-off assessment:

  • VIX < 18 → bullish signal (risk-on)
  • VIX > 25 → bearish signal (risk-off)
  • 18-25 → neutral

VIX Weekly Trend

The dashboard also computes VIX weekly change from a 5-day chart. A rapidly rising VIX (e.g., +20% in a week) is an early warning that equity stress is building — even before VIX crosses the 30 threshold. This provides lead time to reduce positions.

The Spillover Mechanism

When VIX spikes, it typically means:

  1. Institutional risk managers are de-risking across all asset classes
  2. Margin calls in equities force liquidation of other positions (including BTC)
  3. Correlation between BTC and equities increases during stress (everything sells off together)
Correlation breakdown: During acute risk-off (e.g., COVID March 2020), BTC can decouple from equities for weeks. The macro layer assumes BTC eventually re-correlates — but during breakdowns, structural and derivatives layers matter more.
