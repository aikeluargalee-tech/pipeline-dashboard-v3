Volume Profile: Finding POC, VAH, and VAL

Structural Liquidity · 7 min read · Part of the structural liquidity cluster

Price tells you what Bitcoin costs. Volume profile tells you where the market actually traded — and those price levels become magnets, support, and resistance. The Pipeline Dashboard builds volume profile from 7 days of 1-hour Binance klines.

What Volume Profile Shows

Instead of plotting volume against time (the familiar bar chart at the bottom), volume profile plots volume against price. The result is a horizontal histogram showing how much BTC traded at each price level. Price levels with high volume are "accepted" by the market — they act as magnets. Price levels with low volume are "rejected" — price passes through them quickly.

The Three Key Levels

POC (Point of Control)

The single price level with the highest trading volume. This is the market's consensus "fair value" — the price where the most buyers and sellers agreed. Price tends to revisit the POC when it's away from it, and the POC acts as strong support/resistance when price is near it.

VAH (Value Area High)

The upper boundary of the Value Area — the price range containing 70% of total volume. VAH acts as resistance. When price breaks above VAH, it signals acceptance of higher prices.

VAL (Value Area Low)

The lower boundary of the Value Area. VAL acts as support. When price breaks below VAL, it signals acceptance of lower prices — unless volume declines and CVD holds, which signals VAL Absorption.

How the Dashboard Computes It

The generate_volume_profile() function in collect.py:

  1. Fetches 168 hourly klines (7 days) from Binance
  2. Distributes each candle's volume across $50 price bins
  3. Identifies POC as the bin with highest volume
  4. Expands outward from POC until 70% of volume is captured (Value Area)
  5. Identifies HVNs (top 5 high-volume nodes) and LVNs (bottom 3 low-volume nodes)

Trading with Volume Profile

  • Price at POC: Fair value — range-bound trading expected
  • Price above VAH: Bullish — market accepting higher prices
  • Price below VAL: Bearish — unless VAL Absorption detected
  • Price at LVN: Fast moves expected — low volume means little resistance
