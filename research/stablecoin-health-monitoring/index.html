Stablecoin Health: Why USDT/USDC Depeg Matters

Risk Frameworks · 5 min read

Stablecoins are the plumbing of crypto. When USDT or USDC loses its dollar peg, it's like the pipes bursting — everything connected gets damaged. Gate0's stablecoin module monitors peg stability as a crypto-specific systemic risk indicator.

Why Stablecoins Matter for BTC

Most Bitcoin trading pairs are quoted in stablecoins (BTCUSDT, BTCUSDC). If the stablecoin depegs, the BTC price you see is distorted — a USDT at $0.95 makes BTC appear 5% cheaper than it is. More importantly, depeg events trigger panic selling across the entire crypto market, including BTC.

Historical Depeg Events

  • UST collapse (May 2022): Terra's algorithmic stablecoin depegged from $1 to $0.01. BTC dropped 30% in a week as the contagion spread.
  • USDC depeg (March 2023): SVB bankruptcy caused USDC to drop to $0.87. BTC temporarily spiked on USDC pairs (distorted price) before correcting.
  • USDT brief depeg (June 2022): USDT dropped to $0.95 during the Celsius crisis. Brief but alarming.

Gate0 Thresholds

The stablecoin module monitors each major stablecoin's deviation from $1:

  • Deviation > 2% → ABORT: Severe depeg. The dollar backing crypto trades is broken. Flatten everything.
  • Deviation > 0.5% → PAUSE: Significant stress. No new positions.
  • Deviation < 0.5% → PROCEED: Normal.

How It's Monitored

The dashboard reads stablecoin state from /tmp/btc_stablecoin_state.json, which tracks USDT and USDC market cap trends and price deviations. The Gate0 module iterates through all tracked stablecoins and uses the most restrictive state.

Early warning: Stablecoin depegs often precede broader crypto market stress. If USDT starts drifting even 0.3% from $1, it's worth paying attention — the 0.5% PAUSE threshold may not be far behind.
