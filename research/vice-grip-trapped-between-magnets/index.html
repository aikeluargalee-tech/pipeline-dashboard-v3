Vice Grip: When Bitcoin Is Trapped Between Magnets

Structural Liquidity · 5 min read

The Vice Grip is the most volatile of the four liquidation magnet regimes. It occurs when Bitcoin's price is compressed within $500 of both the above and below liquidation clusters simultaneously. Something has to give — and it usually does, violently.

What Causes a Vice Grip

Liquidation clusters form where leveraged traders have their liquidation prices. When both the above cluster (short liquidations) and below cluster (long liquidations) are within $500 of current price, price is "trapped" — any move triggers cascading liquidations in one direction.

How the Dashboard Detects It

The collect_structural() function computes sandwich geometry from heatmap data. When width_usd ≤ 500 (the distance between above and below magnets), the regime is classified as Vice Grip.

Trading Implications

High risk: A Vice Grip means a large move is imminent, but the direction is uncertain. This is not the time for large positions — it's the time to wait for direction confirmation.
  • Reduce position size — volatility is about to spike
  • Wait for breakout — let the cascade happen, then enter on retest
  • Watch CVD — the direction of taker flow often predicts which way the cascade goes
