VAL Absorption: Detecting Smart Money Accumulation

Structural Liquidity · 8 min read

When price drops to the Value Area Low, two things can happen: it breaks through (bearish) or it gets absorbed (bullish). VAL Absorption is the dashboard's active signal monitor that detects the latter — institutional accumulation at a key support level.

The Setup

VAL Absorption requires three conditions to align:

  1. Price near or below VAL: Price is within 3% of the Value Area Low or below it
  2. Volume declining: Late 4-hour volume is less than 85% of early 4-hour volume — selling pressure is fading
  3. CVD holding: Cumulative Volume Delta is non-negative — buyers are still present despite the price drop

The Signal Types

The detect_val_absorption() function produces four signal types:

  • VAL_ABSORPTION (HIGH/MEDIUM confidence): All three conditions met. Volume declining + CVD holding = buyers absorbing seller pressure. Watch for reclaim inside the Value Area for long entry.
  • VAL_BREAK (HIGH confidence): Volume expanding + CVD falling. Real distribution — structure has failed. Bearish.
  • VAL_TEST (LOW confidence): Price testing VAL but volume/CVD not yet confirming. Wait.
  • VAL_APPROACH (LOW confidence): Price nearing VAL but not yet in range. Monitoring.

Entry Logic

When VAL_ABSORPTION fires, the signal includes:

  • Target: VAH (Value Area High) — the top of the value area
  • Stop loss: 1% below VAL
  • Confidence: HIGH if CVD is strengthening, MEDIUM otherwise

Why This Works

When price drops to VAL and volume declines, it means sellers are exhausted — they're running out of coins to sell. If CVD is still positive, it means buyers are quietly absorbing the remaining sell pressure. This is the footprint of institutional accumulation: large buyers who don't want to push price up, so they absorb sells passively.

Opposite signal: If volume is expanding and CVD is falling at VAL, that's VAL_BREAK — real distribution. The structure has failed and price is likely to continue lower.
