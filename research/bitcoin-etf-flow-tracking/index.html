Bitcoin ETF Flows: Tracking Institutional Capital

Macro Context · 6 min read

Since the approval of Bitcoin spot ETFs in January 2024, ETF flows have become a primary driver of BTC price. Daily net inflows indicate institutional capital entering; sustained outflows indicate institutional exit. The dashboard tracks daily and weekly net flows.

What ETF Flows Show

Spot ETFs (IBIT, FBTC, GBTC, ARKB, BITB, and others) allow traditional investors to gain Bitcoin exposure through regulated stock market instruments. When these ETFs see net inflows, it means new institutional capital is entering Bitcoin. When they see net outflows, capital is leaving.

Dashboard Tracking

The dashboard tracks ETF flows from Farside.co.uk, parsing daily flow data for all major BTC ETFs. It computes:

  • Daily net flow: Net inflow or outflow for the most recent trading day
  • Weekly net flow: Sum of the last 5 trading days
  • Trend: inflow, mild outflow, or heavy outflow based on 3-day cumulative

Trend Classification

  • Inflow: 3-day cumulative is positive — institutions are buying
  • Heavy outflow: 3-day cumulative below -$200M — institutions are exiting aggressively
  • Mild outflow: 3-day cumulative negative but above -$200M — modest selling

Why It Matters

ETF flows represent the single largest source of new demand for Bitcoin since 2024. When flows are consistently positive, price tends to rise. When flows turn negative for multiple days, price pressure builds. The ETF flow trend is one of four signals in the macro regime classification.

Field mapping: The dashboard's map_etf_fields() function handles multiple source formats — different data providers use different field names for the same data. This ensures the flow data is correctly parsed regardless of upstream format changes.
