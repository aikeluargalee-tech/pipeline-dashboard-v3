Long/Short Ratios: What Top Traders Are Doing

Derivatives · 5 min read

The long/short ratio reveals how top trader accounts are positioned in Bitcoin futures. It's a positioning metric — not a signal on its own, but context that helps you understand who's leaning which way and how crowded the trade is.

What the Ratio Means

The dashboard fetches Binance Futures' global long/short account ratio for top traders. A ratio of 2.0 means twice as many top traders are long as short. A ratio of 0.5 means twice as many are short.

Reading the Ratio

  • > 2.0: Longs crowded. High squeeze risk — if price drops, over-leveraged longs will be liquidated, amplifying the decline.
  • 1.5-2.0: Balanced bullish. Normal bull market positioning.
  • 1.0-1.5: Balanced. No clear lean.
  • < 1.0: Shorts crowded. Short squeeze risk — if price rises, shorts will be liquidated.

Dashboard Display

The Funding & Order Flow card shows the L/S ratio alongside the percentage of longs and shorts. For example: "L/S 1.89, Longs 65.4%, Shorts 34.6%" means 65.4% of top trader accounts are long.

Crowded Positioning Risk

Squeeze risk: When the ratio is extreme (> 2.0 or < 0.5), the crowded side is vulnerable to a squeeze. A move against the crowd triggers cascading liquidations. This is why the dashboard combines L/S ratio with funding rates — both must agree for a squeeze setup.
