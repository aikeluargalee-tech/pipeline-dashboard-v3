Signal Tracking: WIN, LOSS, EXPIRED Methodology

Trading Execution · 7 min read

Signals without outcome tracking are just opinions. The Pipeline Dashboard tracks every signal it generates through to resolution — WIN, LOSS, or EXPIRED — and uses the results to calibrate confidence labels. This creates an auditable, data-driven track record.

The Three Outcomes

  • WIN: Price reached the target before the stop loss. For LONGs: price >= target. For SHORTs: price <= target.
  • LOSS: Price hit the stop loss before the target. For LONGs: price <= stop. For SHORTs: price >= stop.
  • EXPIRED: Neither target nor stop was hit within 48 hours. The signal didn't play out — the market didn't move enough in either direction.

The 48-Hour Window

Every signal has a 48-hour evaluation window from the moment it fires. This is long enough for the setup to play out but short enough to remain relevant. After 48 hours, unresolved signals are marked EXPIRED with the current price as the outcome price.

How Tracking Works

The track_outcomes() function in detect_only.py runs every 15 minutes:

  1. Reads signal history from /tmp/btc_signal_history.json
  2. Fetches current BTC price from data/btc_price.json
  3. For each unresolved signal, checks if price has hit target (WIN) or stop (LOSS)
  4. If signal is older than 48h, marks it EXPIRED
  5. Writes updated outcomes back to history

Direction-Aware Logic

The tracking logic is direction-aware:

  • LONG signals: WIN = price >= target, LOSS = price <= stop
  • SHORT signals: WIN = price <= target, LOSS = price >= stop

This is why the direction:LONG fix was critical — without it, breakout entry signals were tracked as SHORT, recording instant false WINs (target above entry → "price <= target" was immediately true).

Confidence Calibration

Signal outcomes feed back into confidence calibration:

  • HIGH: ≥ 70% hit rate over 90 days, ≥ 30 trades
  • MEDIUM: 50-70% over 90 days, ≥ 15 trades
  • LOW: < 50% or < 15 trades

If a HIGH label drops below 65% for 30 days, it's auto-downgraded to MEDIUM.

Dashboard Display

The signal stats footer shows the running tally: wins, losses, expired, and open signals, plus the win rate percentage. This gives you a quick read on how the system is performing.
