Bitcoin Cycle Phases: Accumulation to Distribution

On-Chain Valuation · 7 min read

Bitcoin doesn't move in a straight line — it cycles. The Pipeline Dashboard classifies the current cycle phase using MVRV Z-Score thresholds, giving you a quick read on where we are in the 4-year cycle.

The Five Phases

The dashboard's classify_cycle_regime() function maps MVRV Z-Score to five phases:

  • ACCUMULATION (Z < 0.1): Market cap is at or below realized cap. Holders are underwater. Smart money is accumulating. Historically the best risk/reward zone for long-term entries.
  • EARLY BULL (0.1 ≤ Z ≤ 0.5): The cycle is turning. Market cap has moved slightly above realized cap. Early signs of recovery.
  • MID BULL (0.5 < Z < 2.0): Normal bull market. Holders are in profit but not excessively. The trend is your friend.
  • OVERHEATED (2.0 ≤ Z < 4.0): Market cap is significantly above realized cap. Profit-taking is increasing. Historical warning zone — prepare for potential correction.
  • CYCLE TOP (Z ≥ 4.0): Extreme overvaluation. Every previous instance preceded a 50-80% correction. Reduce exposure.

The 4-Year Cycle Thesis

Bitcoin's cycle is driven by the halving — the event that cuts miner rewards in half every ~4 years. The supply shock creates a predictable pattern: accumulation → bull run → top → bear market → accumulation. The 2012, 2016, 2020, and 2024 halvings all followed this pattern.

Structural break risk: The spot ETF era (post-2024) may change demand dynamics in ways the 4-year cycle model doesn't anticipate. Treat cycle phases as context, not prophecy.

How Phases Feed the Synthesis

Cycle phase is displayed in the On-Chain Valuation card and feeds into the regime synthesis. However, cycle data gets 0.5x weight — it's monthly-scale and cannot outvote live structural events. A CYCLE TOP reading doesn't automatically produce a bearish verdict if structure and derivatives are bullish.
