Breakdown-Retest: The Bearish Mirror Setup

Trading Execution · 7 min read

The breakdown-retest is the bearish mirror of the breakout-retest. Price breaks below support, pulls back up to test the broken level from below, and gets rejected. The dashboard tracks this setup through the same lifecycle — but with inverted logic and a critical CVD filter.

The Four Stages

Stage 1: BREAKDOWN_DETECTED

Price breaks below 4H support with seller confirmation:

  • Taker buy ratio < 0.8 (aggressive sellers)
  • CVD < 0 (negative cumulative flow)
  • No magnet cluster within 0.5% below (would cause a bounce sweep)
  • Minimum volume during thin hours
  • At least 0.5x ATR of space below to next support
  • Support not within 1% of VAL (VAL Absorption handles that zone)

Stage 2: RETEST_ACTIVE

Price pulls back up to within 0.3% of the broken support level from below. The system watches for rejection confirmation.

Stage 3: ENTRY_SIGNAL

Price drops 0.3% below the level, and the 1-hour candle closes below. Rejection confirmed. But there's a critical filter:

CVD flip filter: If CVD has turned positive during the retest, the signal is SKIPPED with status "cvd_flip_skip." Buyers have stepped in — this is VAL Absorption territory, not a breakdown. You don't short when buyers are absorbing.

If CVD is still negative, the entry signal fires:

  • Direction: SHORT
  • Stop loss: 1% above the broken level
  • Target: Next support below, or below magnet
  • Confidence: HIGH if price is 1%+ below level, MEDIUM otherwise

Stage 4: Outcome Tracking

Same WIN/LOSS/EXPIRED tracking as breakout-retest, but with inverted logic: WIN = price <= target, LOSS = price >= stop.

The VAL Exclusion Zone

The breakdown-retest detector explicitly skips support levels within 1% of VAL. This prevents overlap with the VAL Absorption monitor — if price is at VAL, the VAL Absorption detector handles it, not the breakdown detector.
