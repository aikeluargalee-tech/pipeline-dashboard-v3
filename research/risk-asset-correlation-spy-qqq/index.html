Risk Assets: SPY, QQQ, and Bitcoin Correlation

Macro Context · 5 min read

Bitcoin doesn't trade in isolation. During normal market conditions, it correlates with equity indices — especially the tech-heavy Nasdaq (QQQ). The dashboard tracks SPY, QQQ, and GLD alongside BTC to provide macro context.

The Three Risk Assets

  • SPY (S&P 500): Broad US equity market. When SPY drops sharply, BTC typically follows — institutional risk-off affects both.
  • QQQ (Nasdaq-100): Tech-heavy index. BTC's correlation with QQQ is typically stronger than with SPY — both are "high-beta" risk assets.
  • GLD (Gold): Safe haven. When gold rallies while equities fall, it signals risk-off — which can be bearish for BTC in the short term but bullish long-term (store of value narrative).

Dashboard Display

The Risk Assets card shows each asset's price and daily change percentage. The dashboard fetches these from Yahoo Finance with a fallback: if the cached data has NaN values, it fetches directly from Yahoo's API.

Correlation Dynamics

Correlation increases during stress: During normal markets, BTC-equity correlation is moderate (0.3-0.5). During risk-off events, correlation spikes toward 1.0 — everything sells off together. This is why VIX > 30 triggers a Gate0 PAUSE.

SPX Change as Gate0 Input

The Gate0 VIX/SPX module uses SPX change (from SPY or QQQ) alongside VIX to assess equity stress. If SPY is falling while VIX is rising, it confirms risk-off conditions that will likely impact BTC.
