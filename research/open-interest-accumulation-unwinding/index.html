Open Interest: Accumulation vs Unwinding

Derivatives · 6 min read

Open Interest (OI) is the total number of outstanding futures contracts. It tells you whether new money is entering the market or existing positions are being closed. Combined with price direction, OI reveals the market's underlying dynamics.

The Four Scenarios

  • Rising OI + Rising price: New longs entering. Bullish — fresh capital is driving the uptrend.
  • Rising OI + Falling price: New shorts entering. Bearish — fresh capital is betting against BTC.
  • Falling OI + Rising price: Short covering. Moderately bullish — but the move may be unsustainable (no new longs).
  • Falling OI + Falling price: Long unwinding. Bearish — positions are being closed, not new shorts opening.

Dashboard Trend Detection

The dashboard fetches 24 hours of hourly OI data and compares the last 6 hours to the first 6 hours:

  • Accumulating: Late OI > 2% above early OI — new money entering
  • Unwinding: Late OI > 2% below early OI — positions closing
  • Stable: OI roughly unchanged

This trend badge appears on the Open Interest card alongside the absolute OI value in BTC and USD.

OI Change Percentage

The dashboard also computes a 24h OI change percentage — comparing the first and last data points. This gives a quick read on how much positioning has shifted in the last day.

Cascade Risk

High OI combined with extreme funding rates creates cascade risk — the conditions for a liquidation cascade. When OI exceeds $8B and funding is above 0.03%, the dashboard flags HIGH cascade risk. A small price move could trigger cascading liquidations.
