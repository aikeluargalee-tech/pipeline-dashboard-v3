SOPR: When Bitcoin Holders Spend at a Loss

On-Chain Valuation · 6 min read · Part of the on-chain valuation cluster

SOPR (Spent Output Profit Ratio) answers a simple question: are Bitcoin holders selling their coins at a profit or a loss? It's one of three on-chain metrics feeding the Pipeline Dashboard's composite cycle score, alongside MVRV Z-Score and Puell Multiple.

What SOPR Measures

SOPR is the ratio of realized value to spent value for all coins that moved on a given day. In plain terms: it divides the price at which coins were sold by the price at which they were last bought. A SOPR of 1.0 means holders are breaking even. Above 1.0 means they're selling at a profit. Below 1.0 means they're selling at a loss.

Reading the Dashboard

The Pipeline Dashboard classifies SOPR into four zones:

  • Below 0.95 (Capitulation): Holders are selling at significant losses. This historically marks cycle bottoms — the people who give up at a loss are the ones who fuel the recovery.
  • 0.95 to 1.0 (Neutral spending): Holders are roughly breaking even. Market is in equilibrium.
  • 1.0 to 1.05 (Normal profit-taking): Healthy bull market behavior. Some holders take profits, but not aggressively.
  • Above 1.05 (Aggressive profit-taking): Holders are selling heavily into strength. This can signal a local top — too many sellers at once.

How SOPR Feeds the Composite Score

In the dashboard's compute_composite_score() function, SOPR is normalized to a 0-100 scale:

  • 0.6-0.8 → 0-20 (deep capitulation, low score)
  • 0.8-1.0 → 20-40 (near breakeven)
  • 1.0-1.5 → 40-70 (normal to elevated profit-taking)
  • 1.5-2.0 → 70-100 (aggressive profit-taking, high score)

The composite score averages this with MVRV Z-Score and Puell Multiple to produce a single cycle positioning metric.

The SOPR Reset Pattern

During bull markets, SOPR typically oscillates above 1.0. When it dips to exactly 1.0 and bounces, it's called a "SOPR reset" — holders who were underwater have finished selling, and the market can resume its uptrend. Multiple SOPR resets during a bull market are healthy.

When SOPR drops below 1.0 and stays there, it signals a regime shift — the market has turned bearish and holders are consistently selling at a loss.

Relationship to MVRV: While MVRV measures the potential for profit-taking (how far market cap is above realized cap), SOPR measures actual profit-taking (what holders are doing right now). Together they give a complete picture.

Limitations

  • SOPR is daily data — it lags intraday moves
  • It doesn't distinguish between small and large holders — a whale selling at a loss has different implications than retail
  • During extreme volatility, SOPR can spike or crash rapidly, making interpretation harder
