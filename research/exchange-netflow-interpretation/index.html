Exchange Netflow: Reading Bitcoin Inflows and Outflows

On-Chain Valuation · 5 min read

When Bitcoin moves to an exchange, it's usually about to be sold. When it moves off an exchange, someone is taking it to cold storage — accumulating for the long term. Exchange netflow tracks this supply/demand dynamic over 7 days.

What Netflow Measures

Netflow is the difference between Bitcoin flowing into exchanges (inflows) and flowing out (outflows) over a 7-day period. The Pipeline Dashboard tracks this as exchange_netflow_7d_btc in the cycle layer.

Interpretation

  • Negative netflow (outflows exceed inflows): Bullish. More coins leaving exchanges than arriving — holders are moving to cold storage, reducing available sell supply.
  • Positive netflow (inflows exceed outflows): Bearish. More coins arriving at exchanges — holders are preparing to sell, increasing available supply.

On the Dashboard

The dashboard displays netflow in the On-Chain Valuation card. Negative values are colored green (bullish), positive values red (bearish). The value is in BTC — for example, "-2,940 BTC" means 2,940 more BTC left exchanges than arrived over 7 days.

Why 7 Days?

Daily netflow is noisy — a single large transfer can skew the signal. The 7-day window smooths out daily volatility while remaining responsive enough to catch regime shifts within a week.

Context matters: Large outflows during a price dump can signal capitulation (holders giving up and moving to cold storage in despair). Large inflows during a rally can signal profit-taking. Always read netflow alongside price action.
