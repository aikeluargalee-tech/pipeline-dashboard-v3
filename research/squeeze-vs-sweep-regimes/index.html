Upside Squeeze vs Downside Sweep: Reading Liquidity Regimes

Structural Liquidity · 6 min read

When liquidation magnets are asymmetric — one much closer than the other — price has a directional bias. The Pipeline Dashboard detects this and labels it as Upside Squeeze or Downside Sweep. These regimes directly cap the synthesis ceiling.

Upside Squeeze

The above magnet (short liquidations) is much closer than the below magnet. Specifically, the above distance is less than 50% of the below distance. Price is likely to be pulled upward as short positions get liquidated — a short squeeze.

Trading bias: Favor long setups. Breakout-retest signals are higher probability.

Downside Sweep

The below magnet (long liquidations) is much closer. The below distance is less than 50% of the above distance. Price is likely to be pulled downward as long positions get liquidated — a long squeeze.

Trading bias: Favor short setups. Breakdown-retest signals are higher probability. Avoid new longs.

The Monitor Ceiling

Verdict ceiling enforcement: Active Downside Sweep and Upside Squeeze regimes cap the synthesis ceiling. A bullish verdict cannot coexist with an active Downside Sweep — the monitor tells the truth, and synthesis listens. Similarly, a bearish verdict cannot coexist with an active Upside Squeeze.

This is the most important design decision in the synthesis logic. It means live structural events override cycle and macro context. A bullish MVRV reading doesn't matter if longs are about to get liquidated.

How the Dashboard Computes It

The collect_structural() function compares above_dist and below_dist:

  • If above_dist < below_dist * 0.5 → Upside Squeeze
  • If below_dist < above_dist * 0.5 → Downside Sweep
  • If width_usd ≤ 500 → Vice Grip (overrides both)
  • Otherwise → Balanced
