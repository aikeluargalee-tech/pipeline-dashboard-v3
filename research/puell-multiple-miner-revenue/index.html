Puell Multiple: Miner Revenue as a Cycle Indicator

On-Chain Valuation · 6 min read · Part of the on-chain valuation cluster

Miners are the sellers of first resort in Bitcoin. They must sell coins to cover electricity, hardware, and operational costs. The Puell Multiple tracks whether miners are earning more or less than usual — and when their revenue is depressed, they're forced to sell, creating capitulation bottoms.

What the Puell Multiple Measures

The Puell Multiple is daily miner revenue divided by the 365-day moving average of miner revenue. When the ratio is high, miners are earning far more than their yearly average — they have excess coins to sell, which can create overhead supply. When the ratio is low, miners are earning less than average — they may be forced to liquidate holdings at a loss to stay operational.

Reading the Dashboard

  • Below 0.5 (Miner capitulation): Miner revenue is less than half the yearly average. Historically a strong buy signal — miners are struggling, and their forced selling creates the final flush before a recovery.
  • 0.5 to 2.0 (Normal): Miner revenue is within a normal range relative to the yearly average.
  • 2.0 to 4.0 (Elevated): Miners are earning significantly more than average. Increased selling pressure possible.
  • Above 4.0 (Overheated): Miner revenue is 4x+ the yearly average. Historically marks cycle tops — miners have strong incentive to sell into strength.

How It Feeds the Composite Score

In the dashboard's cycle layer, the Puell Multiple is normalized to 0-100:

  • 0-0.5 → 10-30 (capitulation zone, low score = buy zone)
  • 0.5-2.0 → 30-60 (normal range)
  • 2.0-4.0 → 60-80 (elevated)
  • 4.0+ → 80-100 (overheated, high score = sell zone)

The Halving Effect

Bitcoin halvings cut miner revenue in half overnight. The 2024 halving reduced the block reward from 6.25 to 3.125 BTC. This means the Puell Multiple's baseline shifts after each halving — comparisons across halving cycles should account for this structural break.

Known limitation: The cycle model assumes 4-year halving cycles. A structural break (e.g., spot ETF era changing the demand dynamics) may re-base valuations in ways the Puell Multiple doesn't anticipate.
