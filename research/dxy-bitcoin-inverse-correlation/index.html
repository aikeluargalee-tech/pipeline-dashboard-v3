DXY and Bitcoin: The Inverse Correlation

Macro Context · 6 min read · Part of the macro cluster

The US Dollar Index (DXY) is the most reliable macro correlate for Bitcoin. When the dollar strengthens, BTC typically weakens. When the dollar weakens, BTC typically rallies. This inverse relationship is the foundation of the dashboard's macro layer.

What DXY Measures

DXY measures the US dollar's value against a basket of six major currencies (Euro, Japanese Yen, British Pound, Canadian Dollar, Swedish Krona, Swiss Franc). A rising DXY means the dollar is strengthening globally; a falling DXY means it's weakening.

Dashboard Thresholds

  • DXY < 100: BTC tailwind. Weak dollar makes risk assets more attractive.
  • DXY > 104: BTC headwind. Strong dollar pressures risk assets.
  • 100-104: Neutral zone.

Why the Correlation Exists

Bitcoin is priced in dollars. When the dollar strengthens, it takes fewer dollars to buy the same amount of BTC — price drops. But the relationship goes deeper: a strong dollar often coincides with tight monetary policy (high rates, reduced liquidity), which is bearish for all risk assets including BTC.

In the Macro Regime Classification

DXY is one of four signals in the classify_macro_regime() function. DXY < 100 adds a bullish signal; DXY > 104 adds a bearish signal. Combined with VIX, yields, and ETF flows, these produce the macro regime label: risk-on, risk-off, or mixed.

Correlation breakdown: During acute risk-off events, BTC can decouple from DXY for weeks. The macro layer assumes eventual re-correlation, but during breakdowns, structural and derivatives signals take priority.
