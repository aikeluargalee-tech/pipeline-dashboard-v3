M2 Money Supply: Liquidity and Bitcoin

Macro Context · 5 min read

M2 money supply measures the total amount of money in circulation — cash, checking deposits, savings, and money market funds. It's the broadest measure of liquidity available for investment. When M2 expands, risk assets like Bitcoin benefit. When it contracts, they suffer.

Why M2 Matters for Bitcoin

Bitcoin is a liquidity-sensitive asset. When there's more money in the system, some of it flows to risk assets — including BTC. When money supply contracts (central bank tightening, quantitative tightening), capital leaves risk assets and returns to safe havens.

Historical Pattern

The 2020-2021 Bitcoin bull run coincided with the largest M2 expansion in history — pandemic stimulus injected trillions into the economy. The 2022 bear market coincided with M2 contraction as the Fed tightened. This correlation is why M2 is tracked as a macro context indicator.

Dashboard Display

M2 supply is displayed in the Macro Conditions card in billions of dollars. The dashboard tracks the absolute value and uses it as context for the macro regime classification — expanding M2 supports risk-on conditions.

Limitations

  • M2 is reported monthly — it's a slow-moving indicator, not real-time
  • The relationship is directional, not precise — M2 expansion doesn't guarantee BTC will rise
  • Other factors (DXY, yields, VIX) can override the M2 signal
